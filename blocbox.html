OVERVIEW



Overview: Blocbox is a Software as a Service (SaaS) web application which allows users to collect information and store it in a single place. A user can create documents, store images, links to websites, and collaborate with others in real-time.



Problem: There is no single solution that provides users with the ability to save links, images, and notes all in one place, allowing users to easily access the data from any device, and share and collaborate with other users, or groups of users, in real-time.



Solution: Blocbox is a SaaS, responsive web application which allows users to collect information and store it in a single place. A user can create documents, store images, links to websites, and collaborate with others in real-time. Blocbox offers three pricing tiers (Free, Pro, Expert) which allows users to choose the data storage capacity, amount of collaborators, whether they would like the real-time collaboration feature, and how many folders/groups/boxes they would like to create. This cloud-based solution can be accessed from any device, and it is easy to upgrade at any time.

RESEARCH



USER RESEARCH: User interviews and surveys were conducted to understand how users save, access, and share information such as links, photos, and notes, for both personal and business use cases. The results helped establish user behaviors, what tools were being used, and the pros and cons of the features for each tool.



75% of users say that when it comes to saving content, they utilize tools that are fast and easy, but they seek greater organization of the saved content.



70% of users would like the ability to share saved content with other users or groups, while collaborating in real-time.



80% of users would prefer to use a single cloud-based application for saving information across multiple devices.



50% of users would like the application to include personal and professional account options.





MARKET RESEARCH: A competitive analysis was conducted to understand how competitors such as Box, Dropbox, & Citrix ShareFile are positioning themselves, their primary and target audiences, key differentiators, and what features seemed to attract users. I documented all of the opportunities and weaknesses. Here are some of the key findings:



Many of the competitors were targeting enterprise level users.



Cloud based storage was the most important feature being emphasized.



Rich collaboration features were not provided by competitive solutions.



Many competitors provided enterprise level data security, data governance, and customer support.



My recommendation for Blocbox is to target casual to professional users. The enterprise level user base is oversaturated with competitive solutions. Emphasize the rich sharing and collaboration features as a differentiator. Based on the survey results it would also be beneficial to explore ways to increase organization capabilities and market that functionality as a differentiator.

TESTING



RESULTS: Testing of the interactive prototype provided insightful feedback on the main landing page and the dashboard. The users confirmed their experience with Blocbox was very positive, and described the site as being user-friendly, trustworthy, and organized. I measured the users competency through their success rate of completing different tasks (such as adding a link, or downloading an image), and by monitoring how long it took to complete the tasks. I kept reworking the navigation and placement of elements until users flowed through the tasks seamlessly, and an optimal user experience could be verified.



User testing videos are availabe upon request.





CONCLUSION



TAKEAWAYS & KEY LEARNINGS: Blocbox succeeded in creating an intuitive and responsive application for saving and sharing files. It has a simple design with rich collaboration features that will be sure to please users who are looking for ease of use and greater functionality. The real-time collaboration feature provides the greatest differentiator for the product. Research suggests that users are seeking increased organization features, so it is my recommendation to research how users would like to further organize their information. Though user research was completed throughout the design process, I would have liked to further research how users would like to organize information for both personal and business use cases.
